About us

Bucegi Natural Park

Located somewhere in the heart of Romania, Bucegi Natural Park sits between important urban areas and near the very iconic tourist town, Sinaia, which was the main residence of the Romanian kings before WWII. It was established in 2003 by the Romanian government on an area of 32,500 ha and overlaps the Natura2000 site Bucegi RO SCI 0013. It's home for a large biodiversity of fauna and flora, including endemic species. Many stories of secret tunnels under the mountain and a so-called earth energetic core, have brought our park as one of the most popular natural areas in the country.
